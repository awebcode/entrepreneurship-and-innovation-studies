Section 1: Creativity in Entrepreneurship

Q1. Define creativity in entrepreneurship.2 Marks

Creativity in entrepreneurship is the ability to generate novel and valuable ideas that lead to innovative products, services, or business models by solving problems in unique ways.

Q2. What are the key components of creativity?4 Marks

  • Fluency: Generating numerous ideas
  • Flexibility: Approaching problems from various perspectives
  • Originality: Producing unique and novel ideas
  • Elaboration: Developing and expanding ideas in detail

Q3. Explain the four phases of the entrepreneurial creative process.6 Marks

  1. Background/Knowledge Accumulation: Gathering information through reading, networking, travel, etc.
  2. The Incubation Process: Subconscious processing via relaxation, exercise, and routine activities
  3. The Idea Experience: The "aha" moment when ideas suddenly emerge
  4. Evaluation & Implementation: Testing ideas, seeking feedback, and executing while viewing problems as challenges

Q4. What are the main barriers to creativity?4 Marks

  • Fear of failure
  • Rigid thinking and adherence to tradition
  • Lack of resources (time, funding, support)
  • Unsupportive organizational or environmental culture

Q5. Explain the Six Thinking Hats method.5 Marks

Developed by Edward de Bono, it encourages structured parallel thinking from six perspectives:

  • White Hat: Facts and data
  • Red Hat: Emotions and intuition
  • Black Hat: Risks and caution
  • Yellow Hat: Benefits and optimism
  • Green Hat: Creativity and new ideas
  • Blue Hat: Process control and organization

Q6. What is the SCAMPER technique?3 Marks

SCAMPER is a creative checklist used to innovate existing products/services:

  • Substitute
  • Combine
  • Adapt
  • Modify
  • Put to another use
  • Eliminate
  • Reverse

Section 2: Business Plan

Q7. What is a business plan?3 Marks

A business plan is a written document that describes all relevant internal and external elements and strategies for starting and running a new venture, integrating functional plans for the first three years.

Q8. What is the value of a business plan?4 Marks

  • Determines viability of the venture in the market
  • Guides the entrepreneur in organizing planning activities
  • Serves as a key tool for obtaining financing
  • Provides self-assessment for the entrepreneur

Q9. How do potential investors evaluate business plans?4 Marks

  • Entrepreneur’s character, background, and capability
  • Growth potential and possibility of high returns
  • Market size, competitive advantage, and financial projections
  • Time spent on background checks and due diligence

Q10. What are the key sections of a business plan?6 Marks

  1. Introductory Page
  2. Executive Summary
  3. Environmental and Industry Analysis
  4. Description of the Venture
  5. Operations Plan
  6. Marketing Plan
  7. Organizational Plan
  8. Assessment of Risk
  9. Financial Plan
  10. Appendix

Q11. What should be included in the Marketing Plan?3 Marks

  • Market conditions and target customers
  • Distribution, pricing, and promotion strategies
  • Marketing research evidence
  • Sales forecasting
Important: Investors consider the marketing plan critical to venture success.

Q12. What should be included in the Financial Plan?3 Marks

  • Pro forma income statements and forecasted sales/expenses (3 years)
  • Cash flow projections (3 years)
  • Projected balance sheet
  • Key financial ratios and break-even analysis

Q13. What should be included in the Assessment of Risk?3 Marks

  • Identification of potential hazards and risks
  • Evaluation of their impact
  • Strategies to prevent, minimize, or respond to risks

Q14. Why do some business plans fail?4 Marks

  • Goals are unreasonable or not measurable
  • Lack of total commitment or experience from the entrepreneur
  • No established customer need for the product/service
  • Ignoring potential threats, weaknesses, or market changes

Section 3: Forms of Ownership & Venture Finance

Q15. Compare Sole Proprietorship and Partnership.4 Marks

Sole Proprietorship: Owned by one person; full control; simple setup; unlimited liability; limited resources.

Partnership: Owned by 2+ persons; shared capital/skills; governed by Partnership Act; unlimited liability (general); types include general and limited.

Q16. Compare Private Limited and Public Limited Company.4 Marks

Private Limited: Separate legal entity; limited liability; restricted shareholders; no public share trading; moderate compliance.

Public Limited: Can raise capital from public via shares; strict regulations and high compliance; suitable for large-scale ventures.

Q17. What are the different sources of venture finance?6 Marks

  1. Equity Financing: Angel investors, venture capitalists, IPO
  2. Debt Financing: Bank loans, term loans, working capital loans
  3. Bootstrapping: Personal savings or early revenue
  4. Grants & Subsidies: Government/incubator support
  5. Crowdfunding: Platforms (reward, equity, donation-based)
  6. Trade Credit: Supplier financing/delayed payments

Q18. Difference between Equity and Debt Financing.4 Marks

Equity: Funds from ownership sale; no repayment obligation; investors share profits/risks; dilutes control.

Debt: Loans to be repaid with interest; no ownership dilution; lender has no profit share; fixed repayment schedule.

Q19. What criteria should be considered when selecting venture finance?6 Marks

  • Stage of business (idea, growth, mature)
  • Amount of capital required
  • Cost of capital (interest vs dilution)
  • Desired level of control and ownership
  • Risk tolerance of the entrepreneur
  • Legal and regulatory compliance requirements

Section 4: Bangladesh Context & Core Concepts

Q20. Describe key policy initiatives in Bangladesh to promote entrepreneurship.6 Marks

  • National Industrial Policy 2022: Focus on SMEs, startups, women entrepreneurs, high-tech industries
  • SME Policy: Formalization, capacity building, finance access
  • Digital/Smart Bangladesh Vision: ICT entrepreneurship, freelancing, youth innovation
  • National Youth Policy: Skill development, startup training
  • BIDA One Stop Service: Simplified registration and investment processes

Q21. What are the key institutions supporting entrepreneurship in Bangladesh?6 Marks

  • SME Foundation: Policy advocacy, training, fairs, finance access
  • BSCIC: Industrial estates, technical support
  • Bangladesh Bank: Refinancing, startup funds, women entrepreneur schemes
  • BIDA: Investment facilitation, OSS
  • Startup Bangladesh Limited (ICT Division): Seed funding, mentoring, acceleration

Q22. What is entrepreneurship?3 Marks

Entrepreneurship is the process of identifying opportunities, taking calculated risks, gathering resources, and creating value through innovative new ventures.

Q23. What are the key characteristics of successful entrepreneurs?4 Marks

  • Risk-taking (calculated)
  • Innovation and creativity
  • Persistence and resilience
  • Vision and leadership
  • Adaptability to change

Q24. What are the types of innovation?4 Marks

  • Product Innovation: New/improved products or services
  • Process Innovation: New methods of production/delivery
  • Marketing Innovation: New pricing, promotion, or packaging
  • Organizational Innovation: New business practices or structures

Q25. What is disruptive innovation?3 Marks

Disruptive innovation introduces a product/service that initially serves overlooked markets and eventually displaces established competitors (e.g., Uber disrupting traditional taxis).
