Section 1: Creativity in Entrepreneurship

Q1. Define creativity in entrepreneurship.2 Marks

Creativity = Ability to generate new + valuable ideas that lead to innovative products, services or business models.

Example: Uber created ride-sharing app instead of traditional taxis.

Q2. Key components of creativity.4 Marks

  • Fluency→ Generating many ideas quickly
  • Flexibility→ Seeing problems from different angles/perspectives
  • Originality→ Producing unique and novel ideas
  • Elaboration→ Adding details and developing ideas fully
Example: Brainstorming 20 app ideas (fluency), thinking B2B & B2C (flexibility), inventing something new (originality), adding full features (elaboration).

Q3. Four phases of creative process.6 Marks

  • Knowledge Accumulation→ Gathering information through reading, networking, travel
  • Incubation→ Letting ideas develop subconsciously through relaxation and routine activities
  • Idea Experience→ Sudden "aha!" moment when insight appears
  • Evaluation & Implementation→ Testing the idea, getting feedback, and putting it into action
Example: Research food delivery market → relax → sudden drone delivery idea → test with pilot.

Q4. Main barriers to creativity.4 Marks

  • Fear of failure→ Afraid to take risks or try new things
  • Rigid thinking→ Stuck to old habits and traditional methods
  • Lack of resources→ Insufficient time, money, or support
  • Unsupportive culture→ Environment or organization discourages new ideas
Example: Company punishes failed experiments → employees stop trying new things.

Q5. Six Thinking Hats method.5 Marks

By Edward de Bono – structured thinking using 6 colored hats:

  • White Hat→ Focus only on facts and data
  • Red Hat→ Emotions, feelings, and intuition
  • Black Hat→ Risks, problems, and caution
  • Yellow Hat→ Benefits, positives, and optimism
  • Green Hat→ New ideas, alternatives, and creativity
  • Blue Hat→ Controls the thinking process and agenda
Example: New product launch → White: market data, Black: risks, Green: new features.

Q6. SCAMPER technique.3 Marks

Creative checklist to improve existing products/services:

  • Substitute→ Replace materials, parts, or processes
  • Combine→ Mix with other products or ideas
  • Adapt→ Adjust from something else for new use
  • Modify→ Change size, shape, color, or features
  • Put to another use→ Find new applications
  • Eliminate→ Remove unnecessary parts
  • Reverse→ Rearrange or do the opposite
Example: Improve pen → Substitute plastic with metal, Combine with flashlight, Eliminate cap.

Section 2: Business Plan

Q7. What is a business plan?3 Marks

Written document describing all internal & external elements and strategies for starting and running a new venture (covers first 3 years).

Example: Coffee shop plan includes location, menu, marketing, and finances.

Q8. Value of business plan.4 Marks

  • Checks viability→ Tests if the idea can succeed in the market
  • Guides planning→ Helps organize activities and decisions
  • Helps get funding→ Essential tool for investors and banks
  • Self-assessment→ Lets entrepreneur evaluate own strengths/weaknesses

Q9. How investors evaluate business plans.4 Marks

  • Entrepreneur’s character→ Trust, capability, and past experience
  • High growth & return potential→ Chance for big profits
  • Market size→ Large and growing target market
  • Competitive advantage→ Unique edge over rivals

Q10. Key sections of business plan.6 Marks

  • Executive Summary→ Brief overview of entire plan
  • Industry Analysis→ External trends and competition
  • Description of Venture→ Product/service and business model
  • Operations Plan→ How product/service will be produced/delivered
  • Marketing Plan→ Target customers, pricing, promotion
  • Organizational Plan→ Ownership structure and team
  • Risk Assessment→ Potential problems and solutions
  • Financial Plan→ Projections and funding needs

Q11. Marketing Plan includes.3 Marks

  • Target customers→ Who will buy and market size
  • Pricing, distribution, promotion→ Strategy to reach and sell
  • Sales forecast→ Expected revenue numbers
Note: Most important section for investors!

Q12. Financial Plan includes.3 Marks

  • Sales & expense forecast→ Projected income for 3 years
  • Cash flow→ Money coming in and out (3 years)
  • Balance sheet→ Assets, liabilities, and equity projection

Q13. Assessment of Risk includes.3 Marks

  • Identify risks→ List possible problems
  • Impact analysis→ How serious each risk is
  • Prevention strategies→ Plans to avoid or handle risks

Q14. Why business plans fail.4 Marks

  • Unrealistic goals→ Targets not achievable or measurable
  • No real customer need→ Product/service nobody wants
  • Lack of commitment/experience→ Entrepreneur not fully dedicated or skilled
  • Ignore threats→ Overlook competition or market changes

Section 3: Forms of Ownership & Venture Finance

Q15. Sole vs Partnership.4 Marks

  • Sole Proprietorship→ One owner, full control, unlimited liability, simple setup
  • Partnership→ 2+ owners, shared resources & skills, unlimited liability (general), governed by Partnership Act

Q16. Private vs Public Company.4 Marks

  • Private Limited→ Limited liability, few shareholders, no public trading of shares, moderate regulation
  • Public Limited→ Can raise funds from public via shares, strict compliance and reporting, suitable for large ventures

Q17. Sources of venture finance.6 Marks

  • Equity Financing→ From owners/investors (angels, VC, IPO) – no repayment
  • Debt Financing→ Loans from banks – must repay with interest
  • Bootstrapping→ Using personal savings or early revenue
  • Grants/Subsidies→ Non-repayable government or incubator support
  • Crowdfunding→ Raising small amounts from many people online
  • Trade Credit→ Delayed payment to suppliers

Q18. Equity vs Debt.4 Marks

  • Equity→ No repayment required, but dilute ownership and control
  • Debt→ Must repay with interest, but retain full ownership

Q19. Criteria for choosing finance.6 Marks

  • Business stage→ Idea stage vs growth vs mature
  • Amount needed→ Small vs large capital requirement
  • Cost of capital→ Interest rate vs ownership dilution
  • Control retention→ How much ownership to keep
  • Risk level→ Ability to handle repayment pressure
  • Legal & compliance rules→ Reporting requirements for different sources

Section 4: Bangladesh & Core Concepts

Q20. Key policies in Bangladesh.6 Marks

  • National Industrial Policy 2022→ Focus on SMEs, startups, women entrepreneurs
  • Smart Bangladesh Vision→ Promotes ICT, freelancing, youth innovation
  • SME Policy→ Support for small enterprises and access to finance
  • BIDA One Stop Service→ Simplifies business registration and investment

Q21. Key institutions in Bangladesh.6 Marks

  • SME Foundation→ Training, fairs, finance access for SMEs
  • BSCIC→ Industrial estates and technical support
  • Bangladesh Bank→ Refinancing schemes and startup funds
  • BIDA→ Investment facilitation and one-stop service
  • Startup Bangladesh Ltd→ Seed funding and mentoring for tech startups

Q22. What is entrepreneurship?3 Marks

Process of identifying opportunities, taking calculated risks, and creating value through innovative new ventures.

Q23. Characteristics of entrepreneurs.4 Marks

  • Risk-taking→ Willing to take calculated risks
  • Innovation→ Creating new solutions and ideas
  • Persistence→ Never give up despite obstacles
  • Vision & leadership→ Clear goal and ability to lead team

Q24. Types of innovation.4 Marks

  • Product Innovation→ New or improved products/services
  • Process Innovation→ Better production or delivery methods
  • Marketing Innovation→ New ways of pricing, promotion, packaging
  • Organizational Innovation→ New business practices or structures

Q25. Disruptive innovation.3 Marks

Starts in small/overlooked market with simpler/cheaper solution → eventually displaces established big players.

Example: Uber disrupted traditional taxi industry.
