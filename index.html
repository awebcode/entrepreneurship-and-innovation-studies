Section 1: Creativity in Entrepreneurship

Definition and Components

Q1. Define creativity in entrepreneurship.2 Marks

Creativity in entrepreneurship is the ability to generate novel and valuable ideas that lead to innovative products, services, or business models.

Q2. What are the key components of creativity?4 Marks

  • Fluency: Ability to produce numerous ideas
  • Flexibility: Capacity to approach problems from various perspectives
  • Originality: Skill to generate unique ideas
  • Elaboration: Talent to expand on ideas

The Creative Process

Q3. Explain the four phases of the entrepreneurial creative process.6 Marks

  1. Background/Knowledge Accumulation: Gathering information
  2. The Incubation Process: Subconscious processing
  3. The Idea Experience: The "aha" moment
  4. Evaluation & Implementation: Testing ideas and implementing solutions

Barriers and Promoting Factors

Q4. What are the main barriers to creativity?4 Marks

  • Fear of Failure: Hesitation to take risks
  • Rigid Thinking: Adhering strictly to traditional methods
  • Lack of Resources: Insufficient time, funding, or support
  • Environmental Constraints: Unsupportive organizational culture

Q5. What is the Six Thinking Hats method?5 Marks

A method for looking at problems from six perspectives:

  • White Hat: Focus on data and information
  • Red Hat: Consider emotions and feelings
  • Black Hat: Identify potential problems and risks
  • Yellow Hat: Explore benefits and positives
  • Green Hat: Encourage creativity and new ideas
  • Blue Hat: Manage the thinking process

Section 2: Business Plan

Introduction and Value

Q6. What is a business plan?3 Marks

A written document describing all relevant internal and external elements and strategies for starting a new venture. It addresses short-term and long-term decision-making for the first three years.

Q7. What is the value of a business plan?4 Marks

  • Helps determine the viability of the venture
  • Guides the entrepreneur in organizing planning activities
  • Serves as an important tool in obtaining financing
  • Provides a self-assessment by the entrepreneur

Key Components

Q8. What are the key sections of a business plan?6 Marks

  1. Introductory Page
  2. Executive Summary
  3. Environmental and Industry Analysis
  4. Description of the Venture
  5. Operations Plan
  6. Marketing Plan
  7. Organizational Plan
  8. Assessment of Risk
  9. Financial Plan

Evaluation and Implementation

Q9. How do potential lenders evaluate business plans?4 Marks

Lenders focus on:

  • The venture's ability to pay back debt
  • The four Cs of credit: Character, cash flow, collateral, and equity contribution
  • Objective analysis of business opportunity and risks

Q10. How do potential investors evaluate business plans?4 Marks

Investors focus on:

  • Entrepreneur's character and background
  • Market and financial projections
  • Potential for high rates of return
  • Growth potential of the venture

Section 3: Business Organizations & Venture Finance

Forms of Business Organizations

Q11. Compare Sole Proprietorship, Partnership, and Limited Company.6 Marks

Feature Sole Proprietorship Partnership Limited Company
Ownership Single individual Two or more individuals Shareholders
Liability Unlimited Shared Limited
Formation Simple Partnership agreement Legal registration

Q12. What is a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)?3 Marks

  • Hybrid of partnership and company form
  • Limited liability and legal identity
  • Popular for professionals and small enterprises

Sources of Venture Finance

Q13. Compare Equity Financing and Debt Financing.4 Marks

Feature Equity Financing Debt Financing
Source Owners or shareholders Banks, financial institutions
Repayment No repayment obligation Must be repaid with interest
Ownership Investors share ownership and profits Lender has no ownership stake

Q14. What is bootstrapping in entrepreneurship?3 Marks

  • Using personal savings or revenues from early operations
  • Allows entrepreneurs to maintain full control
  • Forces resourcefulness and lean operations

Policy Initiatives in Bangladesh

Q15. Describe key policy initiatives in Bangladesh to promote entrepreneurship.6 Marks

  • National Industrial Policy 2022: Focuses on promoting SMEs, startups, and women entrepreneurs
  • SME Policy Strategy: Promotes development of SMEs and access to finance
  • Digital Bangladesh Vision 2021: Promotes ICT entrepreneurship and startup ecosystem
  • National Youth Policy: Supports skill development and startup funding

Q16. What are the key institutions supporting entrepreneurship in Bangladesh?6 Marks

Institution Role & Activities

Bangladesh Bank Refinancing schemes for women entrepreneurs
Bangladesh Investment Development Authority (BIDA) Facilitates investment, provides One Stop Service
ICT Division (Startup Bangladesh Limited) Flagship startup platform offering seed funding
