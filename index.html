Section 1: Creativity in Entrepreneurship

Q1. Define creativity in entrepreneurship.2 Marks

Creativity = Ability to generate new + valuable ideas that lead to innovative products, services or business models.

Example: Uber created ride-sharing app instead of traditional taxis.

Q2. Key components of creativity.4 Marks

  • Fluency → Many ideas
  • Flexibility → Different angles/perspectives
  • Originality → Unique/novel ideas
  • Elaboration → Detailed development of ideas
Example: Brainstorming 20 app ideas (fluency), thinking B2B & B2C (flexibility), inventing something new (originality), adding full features (elaboration).

Q3. Four phases of creative process.6 Marks

  • 1. Knowledge Accumulation → Gather info (read, network, travel)
  • 2. Incubation → Let ideas cook subconsciously (relax, exercise, sleep)
  • 3. Idea Experience → "Aha!" moment (sudden insight)
  • 4. Evaluation & Implementation → Test, get feedback, execute
Example: Research food delivery market → relax → sudden drone delivery idea → test with pilot.

Q4. Main barriers to creativity.4 Marks

  • Fear of failure → Afraid to take risks
  • Rigid thinking → Stuck to old/traditional ways
  • Lack of resources → No time, money, or support
  • Unsupportive culture → Organization/environment kills new ideas
Example: Company punishes failed experiments → employees stop trying new things.

Q5. Six Thinking Hats method.5 Marks

By Edward de Bono – 6 colored hats for structured thinking:

  • White Hat → Facts & data only
  • Red Hat → Emotions & feelings
  • Black Hat → Risks & problems
  • Yellow Hat → Benefits & positives
  • Green Hat → New ideas & creativity
  • Blue Hat → Control the process
Example: New product launch → White: market data, Black: risks, Green: new features.

Q6. What is the SCAMPER technique?3 Marks

SCAMPER is a creative checklist used to innovate existing products/services:

  • Substitute - Replace with something else
  • Combine - Mix with other elements
  • Adapt - Adjust for different purpose
  • Modify - Change in some way
  • Put to another use - Use differently
  • Eliminate - Remove parts
  • Reverse - Do it in opposite way

Section 2: Business Plan

Q7. What is a business plan?3 Marks

Written document describing all internal & external elements and strategies for starting a new venture (first 3 years).

Example: Coffee shop plan → location, menu, marketing, finances.

Q8. Value of business plan.4 Marks

  • Checks viability in market
  • Guides planning
  • Helps get funding
  • Self-assessment for entrepreneur

Q9. How investors evaluate business plans.4 Marks

  • Entrepreneur’s character & capability
  • High growth & return potential
  • Market size & financial projections
  • Competitive advantage

Q10. Key sections of business plan.6 Marks

  • Executive Summary
  • Industry Analysis
  • Description of Venture
  • Operations Plan
  • Marketing Plan
  • Organizational Plan
  • Risk Assessment
  • Financial Plan

Q11. Marketing Plan includes.3 Marks

  • Target customers & market
  • Pricing, distribution, promotion
  • Sales forecast
Note: Most important section for investors!

Q12. Financial Plan includes.3 Marks

  • Sales & expense forecast (3 years)
  • Cash flow (3 years)
  • Balance sheet

Q13. Assessment of Risk includes.3 Marks

  • Identify risks
  • Impact analysis
  • Prevention strategies

Q14. Why business plans fail.4 Marks

  • Unrealistic goals
  • No real customer need
  • Lack of commitment/experience
  • Ignore threats

Section 3: Forms of Ownership & Venture Finance

Q15. Sole vs Partnership.4 Marks

  • Sole → 1 owner, full control, unlimited liability, simple
  • Partnership → 2+ owners, shared skills/capital, unlimited liability (general)

Q16. Private vs Public Company.4 Marks

  • Private Ltd → Limited liability, few owners, no public shares
  • Public Ltd → Can sell shares to public, high regulation

Q17. Sources of venture finance.6 Marks

  • Equity → Angels, VC, IPO
  • Debt → Bank loans
  • Bootstrapping → Own savings/revenue
  • Grants/Subsidies → Government
  • Crowdfunding
  • Trade credit

Q18. Equity vs Debt.4 Marks

  • Equity → No repayment, lose some ownership
  • Debt → Must repay + interest, keep full ownership

Q19. Criteria for choosing finance.6 Marks

  • Business stage
  • Amount needed
  • Cost (interest vs dilution)
  • Control retention
  • Risk level
  • Legal rules

Q22. What is entrepreneurship?3 Marks

Identifying opportunities + taking risks + creating value through new innovative ventures.

Q23. Characteristics of entrepreneurs.4 Marks

  • Risk-taking
  • Innovation
  • Persistence
  • Vision & leadership

Q24. Types of innovation.4 Marks

  • Product → New/improved items
  • Process → Better way of doing
  • Marketing → New pricing/promotion
  • Organizational → New structure

Q25. Disruptive innovation.3 Marks

Starts small in overlooked market → eventually displaces big players.

Example: Uber vs traditional taxis.
